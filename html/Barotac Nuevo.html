About Barotac Nuevo

Barotac Nuevo is a municipality in Iloilo, Philippines, known for its love of football and rich cultural heritage. With its historic St. Anthony of Padua Parish Church and vibrant festivals, it's a place full of history and charm.

Festivals

Tamasak Festival

The Tamasak Festival is a vibrant cultural celebration held in Barotac Nuevo, Iloilo, Philippines. It honors the town's rich history and heritage, with a particular focus on its legendary roots. The festival typically features colorful street dances, cultural presentations, and other activities that showcase Barotac Nuevo's local traditions, agricultural abundance, and community spirit. It is also a way to foster pride and unity among its residents while attracting visitors to appreciate its unique cultural identity.

Hili-usa Festival

The Hili-usa Festival is a cultural and religious celebration in Barotac Nuevo, Iloilo, Philippines, showcasing the unity and collective spirit of its people. The name "Hili-usa" comes from the Hiligaynon term meaning "to unite," emphasizing the town's solidarity and cooperation. This festival is held in honor of the town's patron saint, San Antonio de Padua, and features religious activities like processions and novenas. It also highlights the town's history, agricultural heritage, and traditions through street dances, vibrant costumes, and community performances. The Hili-usa Festival reflects Barotac Nuevo's commitment to preserving its cultural identity while fostering a sense of togetherness among its residents.
