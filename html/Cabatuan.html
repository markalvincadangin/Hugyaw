About Cabatuan

Cabatuan is a municipality in Iloilo, Philippines, known for its historical and cultural significance. The town is celebrated for its rich heritage and the unique Tinuom Festival. Cabatuan's culinary traditions, such as the famous Tinuom dish, and its devotion to San Nicolas de Tolentino reflect the spirit and pride of its people.

Festivals

Tinuom Festival

The Tinuom Festival is a culinary and cultural celebration that showcases Cabatuan's signature dish, Tinuom, a hearty chicken soup wrapped and cooked in banana leaves. This festival also honors San Nicolas de Tolentino, the town's patron saint. The Tinuom Festival features colorful street dances, religious activities, and cultural presentations, drawing visitors to experience Cabatuan's unique identity.
